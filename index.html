📊 Round 1 Recap & Analysis

Round 1 is in the books, and the leaderboard didn't quite play to script. Most of the elite favorites went out and shot under par — but the two biggest LIV names laid eggs, completely flipping the pool standings from what most entries were hoping for.

🟢 Heroes of the Day

Rory McIlroy (-5, 67) — The defending champion couldn't have asked for a better opening to his title defense. It's his first sub-70 Thursday at Augusta since 2018, and a massive improvement on last year when he was 7 shots back after Day 1. Notably, this is also the first time since 2011 he's led overnight at Augusta — a tournament he'd rather forget. He's the engine of every entry that picked him.

Patrick Reed (-3, 69) — The biggest value pick of the pool delivered. Reed opened birdie-eagle to become just the sixth player in Masters history to start a tournament that way, then added another eagle on the par-5 8th to turn in 31. Two bogeys on the back kept him from a truly historic round, but he sits T3 — and only 2 entries had him.

Justin Rose (-2, 70) — Reached -4 twice during his round before a bogey-bogey finish dropped him to -2. Still good for a top-10 finish — his 9th in 21 Masters starts (only Jack Nicklaus has more).

Scottie Scheffler (-2, 70) — Eagled the par-5 second and birdied the third to quickly reach -3, then went 14 pars and a bogey the rest of the way. Hit 12 of 14 fairways — his best driving accuracy in 25 rounds. The ball-striking is back.

Tommy Fleetwood (-1, 71) — Quietly steady. Was at -3 through 11 before bogeying his final hole to finish with five birdies against four bogeys.

🔴 Disasters of the Day

Jon Rahm (+6, 78) — Catastrophic. The 2023 champion failed to make a single birdie all day, with four bogeys on the front and a double on the back. He'll need a herculean Friday just to make the cut. This single result is the biggest drag on the pool — 13 entries have him.

Bryson DeChambeau (+4, 76) — A triple bogey on 11 (three shots to escape a bunker) derailed what was an otherwise manageable round. He bogeyed two of his last three holes and is also fighting for the cut. His own lucky bounce off a patron on the par-3 6th saved par; nothing else went his way.

Robert MacIntyre (+8, 80) — Carded a 9 on the par-5 15th after going in the water. The Scotsman flipped off the hole from the fairway in frustration — possibly the most relatable moment of the day.

Ludvig Åberg (+3, 75) — Started on fire with three birdies in his first six holes, then completely unraveled with a double on 9, bogey-bogey on 10–11, and another double on 13. Finished T49. The most popular pick in the pool (19 entries) became one of its biggest drags.

🎯 The Story So Far

Cody Pantages takes the early lead at -11, riding the perfect storm of McIlroy, Rose, Schauffele, and Scheffler. None of his picks blew up. He's the only entry with all four picks under par after Round 1.

Mike Scarr sits 2nd at -9 as the only entry to have picked Patrick Reed. The Reed call alone is what separates him from the rest of the Scarr clan, who all stacked the chalk favorites.

The Scarr brothers Matt and Ryan are tied for 3rd at -6 with the same four picks (Aberg, McIlroy, Schauffele, Scheffler) — would have been better with anyone other than Aberg in their lineup, but McIlroy is doing the heavy lifting.

The bottom of the standings is dominated by entries that doubled up on the LIV pair. Anyone who picked both Rahm and DeChambeau is +10 from those two picks alone, which is nearly impossible to recover from. Five entries are in this trap and sit between +3 and +14.

🔮 What to Watch in Round 2

The cut line — Augusta cuts to the top 50 and ties. Players currently outside that line who appear in pool entries: Rahm (+6), MacIntyre (+8), DeChambeau (+4), and the Aberg/Conners crowd at +3 are all on the bubble. If any of them miss the cut, those entries lose all earning potential from that pick.

Sam Burns — The other co-leader at -5 isn't on a single entry in this pool. If he holds form, he becomes the spoiler nobody picked.

Don't write off any entry yet. Last year's champion (McIlroy) was 7 shots back after Round 1 and still won. With 54 holes remaining, any entry within 10 shots of the lead has a real shot.
